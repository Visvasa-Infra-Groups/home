FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The sale deed is drafted by the buyer/purchaser of the property. The buyer must obtain the documents relating to the property from the seller to ensure the ownership of the seller, property location details and ensure the property is free from any charges. The buyer can draft the sale deed containing all the elements of a sale deed or get it drafted by a legal professional.

A sale deed agreement constitutes the terms and conditions of the sale of a property. It is an agreement between the buyer and seller stating the intention of the buyer to sell the property upon payment of the consideration/sale price by the buyer. The sale deed agreement is the base document upon which the sale deed is drafted. However, it does not involve an immediate transfer of ownership and the buyer has no right and interest in the property upon the execution of the sale deed agreement.

The sale deed is a proof of ownership document of a property. It is a critical document, especially during the sale of the property. It is a legal document used during a property transaction as evidence of the sale and transfer of ownership of a property from the seller to the buyer. It is also referred to as the title deed, final deed or conveyance deed.

The sale deed is valid only after it is registered in the sub-registrar office and signed by the buyer, seller and two witnesses. Once the sale deed is registered, it is valid till the owner sells the property to another person.

If you have lost the original sale deed of the property, you need to file an FIR at the nearest police station. The FIR copy must be submitted to the sub-registrar office along with the application for obtaining the certified copy of the sale deed. The sub-registrar will issue the certified copy of the sale deed in 2-4 weeks.
